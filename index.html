Who am I

About me

I'm ATER at CEREMADE. My research explores how deep learning can be leveraged to model and understand complex physical phenomena.

I received my PhD from Université Paris Dauphine–PSL under the supervision of Boris Haspot and Raphaël Danchin, with funding from the MathInParis2020 doctoral program of the Fondation Sciences Mathématiques de Paris. I previously earned a master’s degree in Mathematical Modeling from Sorbonne Université.

Current Topics

My research

Main focus

[Physics-Aware Deep Learning]

I am currently investigating the interplay between machine learning and physics, with a particular focus on modeling spatio-temporal dynamics. I explore three interconnected questions: How to incorporate prior physical knowledge in machine learning models: How to leverage both prior physical background on a phenomenon and information extracted from data? How can ML models come as a complement to physical ones? More broadly,

Secondary Focus

Foundation Models

I am also exploring domain generalization, foundation models for multiphysics problems, and scalable algorithms for the large-scale deployment of these systems.

Collaboration

This project is being carried out under the supervision of Patrick Gallinari at ISIR, Sorbonne University.
